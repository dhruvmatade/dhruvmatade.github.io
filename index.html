About

I am a PhD Scholar at Dublin City University specializing in Finance and Family Business research. My research focuses on financial decision-making in family firms, particularly examining how varying levels of family ownership influence capital structure decisions. I explore the critical role of institutional quality in shaping financing choices between debt and equity options.

My work also investigates how family firms contribute to democratic processes and examines the distinctive behavioral patterns of family businesses in the Global South.

Beyond academia, I maintain interests in chess, cricket, geopolitics, and cinema, which enrich my analytical approach to business and finance research.

Research Interests

  • Financial decision-making in family firms
  • Capital structure decisions and family ownership levels
  • Institutional quality and financing choices (debt vs. equity)
  • Family firms and democratic contributions
  • Family business behavior in Global South markets
